## Design Context

### Users
Multi-author reading platform with a global audience spanning ages 15–85. Writers publish essays, guides, and long-form articles. Readers browse, bookmark, and track their reading history. The platform must be intuitive for all skill levels and age groups, prioritizing clarity and comfort for extended reading sessions.

### Brand Personality
**Professional · Calm · Sophisticated · Minimal**

The interface should evoke quiet confidence and refined taste. Every element should feel considered, intentional, and respectful of the reader's attention. The platform is a curated space — not a noisy feed.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Layout**: Medium-inspired content hierarchy — generous whitespace, centered reading column, clear visual rhythm
- **Editor**: Notion-like clean input fields and compose experience — understated, focused, distraction-free
- **Buttons & Controls**: Apple ecosystem feel — soft rounded corners, smooth transitions, tactile hover states, subtle depth
- **Overall Aesthetic**: Claude AI-level cleanliness — warm neutrals, soft surfaces, no visual noise, typography-driven

**Color Palette**: Lavender Mist (pastel palette #08)
- Primary tones: `#E7E0F7`, `#D5CCF0`, `#C2B9E9`, `#B0A8E1`, `#9F98D7`, `#8E89CD`
- Used sparingly as accent against warm whites and slate grays

**Anti-references**: Cluttered news sites, aggressive blog layouts with poor spacing, overly saturated or busy color schemes. The design should never feel noisy, dense, or overwhelming.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element should communicate its purpose instantly. No ambiguous icons, cryptic labels, or decorative clutter.
2. **Breathe** — Generous whitespace is not wasted space. Let content breathe and give the eye clear resting points.
3. **Quiet confidence** — Use restraint. A single well-chosen accent color, consistent type scale, and subtle motion convey more sophistication than elaborate decoration.
4. **Universal comfort** — The interface must be effortless for a 15-year-old and a 85-year-old alike. Readable type sizes, clear contrast, obvious interactive affordances.
5. **Consistency is trust** — Every button, input, card, and transition should feel like it belongs to the same family. No one-off styles.
